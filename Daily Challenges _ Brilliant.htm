Melon in the Middle

Two ways we can describe the "center" of a set of numbers are the mean and the median of the set. We can use both to describe how a typical number from the set might look. We calculate them differently, though, so they usually give us different information.

If we have five muskmelons weighing 6,4,2,6,6, 4, 2, 6,6,4,2,6, and 101010 pounds and a giant 929292-pound watermelon, which measure gives us a better idea of a typical melon weight? Keep reading to find out, or jump ahead to today's challenge if you're ready.

To find the mean melon weight, we add the weights and then divide by the number of melons:

6+4+2+6+10+926=1206=20 pounds. \frac{6+4+2+6+10+92}{6} = \frac{120}{6} = 20 \text{ pounds}. 66+4+2+6+10+92​=6120​=20 pounds.

To find the median melon weight, we list the weights in numerical order, and then look for the value in the middle:

24661092. 2 \quad 4 \quad 6 \quad 6 \quad 10 \quad 92. 24661092.

There are an even number of values listed, so we can find the mean of the middle two values: 6+62=122=6\frac{6+6}{2} = \frac{12}{2} = 6 26+6​=212​=6 pounds.

The mean of 202020 pounds and the median of 666 pounds are fairly different values. No melon has a weight near 202020 pounds, while the smaller five melons' weights are all within 444 pounds of the median 666 pounds. In this case, the median gives a better picture of a typical melon weight. This often happens when one value in a data set is much larger or smaller than the rest.

What if we could replace the 929292-pound watermelon with a smaller one? Is there a watermelon that would make the mean and median equal?

Let's call the unknown weight x,x,x, and assume it's less than 444 pounds. A list of all the melons' weights in order is then either

2x46610 2 \quad x \quad 4 \quad 6 \quad 6 \quad 10 2x46610

or

x246610. x \quad 2 \quad 4 \quad 6 \quad 6 \quad 10. x246610.

Either way, the median weight is 4+62=102=5 pounds.\frac{4+6}{2} = \frac{10}{2} = 5 \text{ pounds}.24+6​=210​=5 pounds.

For the mean to also be 555 pounds, we would have

x+2+4+6+6+106=5, \frac{x+2+4+6+6+10}{6} = 5, 6x+2+4+6+6+10​=5,

so

x+286=5. \frac{x+28}{6} = 5.6x+28​=5.

Multiplying both sides of the equation by 666 gives us x+28=30,x + 28 = 30,x+28=30, which means x=2x = 2x=2 pounds.

Are there any other watermelon weights that make the mean and median melon weights equal?

How many different weights are possible for the watermelon?

    from statistics import mean, median

muskmelons = [2, 4, 6, 6, 10]
total_possible = 0

for watermelon in range(0,20):
    mel_mean = mean(muskmelons + [watermelon])
    mel_median = median(muskmelons + [watermelon]) 

    if mel_mean == mel_median:
        total_possible += 1
        print(f"Watermelon: {watermelon} "\
              f"Mean: {mel_mean} Median: {mel_median}")


print(f"Total: {total_possible}")
